Satirical Essay

I thought the year 2019 was bad. Turns out, this year was much worse. This pandemic has spread to all parts of the world. Seven months have passed since the declaration of quarantine. I almost feel like a vampire who has not seen sunlight in a million years. COVID-19 is tough. It won’t just go away. Every day, approximately 2000 cases are reported. In a blink of an eye, a single case turned into almost 400,000 cases in seven months. Various efforts have been made by the Philippine Government. Were their efforts effective in defeating the virus? Our neighboring countries have suppressed the threat of the virus. Why haven’t we?

Surely there is a problem with how the government handles this pandemic. During the first few months of the start of the pandemic, there was a lack of PPE and hospital beds in various healthcare facilities nationwide. This problem somehow has been resolved. Recently, the target number of testings has not been achieved. People need their jobs to fulfill their families’ needs. Businesses have shut down and many jobs have been lost. Sadly, even my favorite branch of Jollibee has shut down. Even the Department of Labor and Employment Secretary, Silvestre Bello III, has admitted that 10 million people may lose their jobs due to the pandemic.

There are some situations that I find unnecessary during the pandemic. The best “joke” that I have heard this pandemic is President Duterte insisting on using kerosene as a disinfectant against COVID-19. Chemists, later on, warns those who actually believe the President that gasoline must not be used as a disinfectant for it is harmful. President Duterte also mentions that the lack of funds is the Philippines’ greatest problem. While funds are the greatest problem right now, PhilHealth officials got caught in allegations that they stole 15 billion pesos. Isn’t health insurance what we most need in a time that we are at risk of getting sick from the virus? Another thing is the rehabilitation of Manila Bay. Why can’t they just do the bare minimum of cleaning up the trash? Is spending 28 million pesos on adding white sand to Manila Bay really worth it? Maybe the long yearned cure for the virus is the white sand after all.

Yes, there are efforts made by the government that are effective and reasonable. There situations mentioned that are not yet resolved. There are also decisions made by the Government that are not necessary nor relevant to the pandemic. Although if I were to write everything, it would create a whole thick book.

Margarette Jollie Caritativo

9 Pinatubo
